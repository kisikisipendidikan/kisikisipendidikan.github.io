11. apa yang dimaksud unsur explicit dan implicit dalam bahasa inggris?


kelas : SMA
Pelajaran : Bahasa Inggris
kategori : opposite meaning
kata kunci : explicit, implicit

pembahasan:
Dalam bahasa Indonesiam unsur explicit sama dengan unsur tersurat. sedangkan unsur implicit adalah unsur tersirat.

Makna kata:
Implicit is indirectly stated or implied.
Explicit is directly stated and spelled out.

contoh pada kalimat:
She said she likes black shoes by saying she likes all colors but white.
Pada kalimat di atas, ia menyukai sepatu hitam. secara tersirat, dia berkata menyukai semua sepatu selain putih

He said, you will not attend that birthday party.
pada kalimat di atas, secara jelas bahwa kamu TIDAK diundang di pesta ulang tahun itu.

37. How do you determine the explicit and implicit ways to connect between two sentences?


Jawaban:

To determine the explicit and implicit ways to connect between two sentences, you can consider the following:

1. Explicit Connections: Explicit connections are directly stated or signaled in the text. They provide clear and obvious links between sentences. Some common explicit connectors include:

  - Coordinating conjunctions: words like "and," "but," "or," "so," "yet," etc.

  - Subordinating conjunctions: words like "because," "although," "while," "if," "when," etc.

  - Transition words: words like "however," "therefore," "in addition," "furthermore," "on the other hand," etc.

2. Implicit Connections: Implicit connections rely on the reader's understanding and interpretation of the context to establish the relationship between sentences. They are not explicitly stated in the text but can be inferred based on the logical flow of ideas. Some examples of implicit connections include:

  - Repetition: when a key word or idea from the previous sentence is repeated in the next sentence, creating a connection between them.

  - Pronoun reference: the use of pronouns like "it," "they," "he," "she," etc., to refer back to a noun or idea mentioned in the previous sentence.

  - Semantic relationships: connections based on the meaning and logical relationship between ideas, such as cause and effect, comparison, contrast, etc.
