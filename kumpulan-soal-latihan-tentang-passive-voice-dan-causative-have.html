1. Buat la kalimat causative have aktif dan passive


Subject : English

Category : Causative  

Level : SHS

Code : 5

Keywords : causative, passive, have

------------

We use causative verb to talk about an action that is done by someone else for us or for another person.  The subject of the sentence caused the action to happen but they paid/told/ordered someone else to do it.

There are two kinds of causative. The active causative and the passive causative.

Active causative :  

Let/make/have someone do something (Let/make/have + direct object + bare infinitive + indirect object)

get someone to do something (get + direct object + to infinitive + indirect object)

Passive causative :

Let/make/have/get something done. (Let/make/have + indirect object + past participle)

---------------

Examples of active causative with the verb have :

1. I have the maid mop the floor.

2. She has the hairdresser dye her hair.

3. My mother had the man carry her shopping.

4. The president had his aides write his speeches.


Examples of passive causative with the verb have :

1. I have the floor mopped (by the maid).

2. She has her hair dyed (by the hairdresser).

3. My mother had her shopping carried (by the man).

4. The president had his speeches written (by his aides).

Passive Voice:

1. Passive Voice [Simple Present Tense]

-Pattern = Subject + to be (is/am/are) + Verb3

2. Passive Voice [Present Continuous Tense]

-Pattern = Subject + to be (is/am/are) + being + Verb3

3. Passive Voice [Present Perfect Tense]

-Pattern = Subject + have/has + been + Verb3

4. Passive Voice [Simple Past Tense]

-Pattern = Subject + to be (was – were) + Verb 3

5. Passive Voice [Past Continuous Tense]

-Pattern = Subject + to be (was – were) + Being + Verb3

6. Passive Voice [Past Perfect Tense]

-Pattern = Subject + had + been + Verb3

7. Passive Voice [Simple Future Tense]

-Pattern = Subject + will + be + Verb3

8. Passive Voice [Future Continuous Tense]

-Pattern = Subject + will be + being + Verb3

9. Passive Voice [Future Perfect Tense]

-Pattern = Subject + will have + been + Verb3

12. they could have broken the windowpassive voice​


The Answer:

The window could have been broken by them.

Explanation

Perintah soal adalah mengubah kalimat aktif ber-modals di soal menjadi bentuk pasifnya.

Untuk mengubahnya menjadi kalimat pasif ber-modals, terlebih dahulu kamu pahami apa itu Modals.

_

Modals merupakan sejenis kata kerja bantu dalam Bahasa Inggris yang digunakan untuk menambahkan makna tambahan pada kata kerja utama dalam kalimat.

Modals terbagi menjadi 2 macam, yaitu: 1) Modal Verbs; dan 2) Modal Phrases. Pada kalimat di soal, jenis Modals yang digunakan adalah Modal Verbs.

_

Rumus kalimat pasif dengan Modal Verbs adalah sebagai berikut.

S + M (+ V₁) + Be + V₃ + by + O

keterangan:

S =  Subjek kalimat pasif.M =  Kata kerja modal (Modal Verb).V₁ =  Kata kerja bentuk pertama.Be =  Kata kerja be atau variasinya.V₃ =  Kata kerja bentuk ketiga.by =  Preposisi untuk menyatakan objek.O =  Objek kalimat pasif.

-

Contoh kalimat dengan struktur tersebut adalah sebagai berikut.

I would be helped by her if only I asked her for help.He could have been replaced by other male singer.

S + has/have + V past participle + O

[tex]~[/tex]

O + have/has + been + V past participle + (by S)

38. I Have got a beautiful present Passive voice


Jawaban:

Answer ⤵️

A sentence is in the active voice when the subject performs an action.

A sentence is said to be in the passive voice when the subject receives an action.

The given sentence is in the active voice.

If this sentence is changed to passive voice, then the subject is also changed, in order to retain the meaning of the sentence.

So,

'Subject (He) + verb (gave) + indirect object (me) + direct object (a beautiful flower pot)' changes to:

'New subject (A beautiful flower pot) + verb (was given) + indirect object (to me) + by + new object (him)'

The passive form of 'gave' in the simple past tense is 'was/were given'.

This can be seen in option A, hence it's the right answer.

Option B: The verb form 'had given to me' is in active form. As a result, the meaning of the sentence is incomplete, as the rest of the sentence is in passive voice.

Option D: Similarly, in this option, the verb form 'had been giving' is in the active form. It expresses the past perfect continuous tense, and does not express the simple past tense of the given sentence.

Option C: This sentence is in the active voice, expressing the past progressive tense. It has changed in meaning.

Hence options B, C and D are incorrect.
